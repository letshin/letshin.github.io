Terabithias Sensors

Our GUI incorporates an interactive floor plan of the vertical farming area. Each module is selectable with a touch-controlled HDI for each growth module and central control node for the entire plant/greenhouse. Current parameters can be visualised and are colour-coded according to their status-check.

Featuring the hub-and-spoke system

Terabithia’s SMART Agro-Theranostic systems uses high-fidelity, robust low-cost low-power modular sensors linked to a central server. It monitors and adjusts for an ideal plant growth environment. Users can toggle between growth modules and sensor clusters from within the GUI. Sensor clusters are connected wirelessly to the server node. 1-sensor-multiple-readout solution ensures fewer sensors used to monitor several parameters simultaneously & introduce cross-checks. Sensor feedback is instantaneous; technical issues can be easily spotted as they are shown in Red.

Electronic Nose

Terabithia’s prototype electronic nose and the current development model of our integrated electronic nose module will further reduce sensors numbers. The e-nose is one of the several sensor and control strategies implemented in SMART. Other systems are simpler but with harder control strategies. The e-nose, spectral control, flow control, nutrient control, feed control etc. which are part of the integrated systems that will be linked to our databases to ensure continual development of the system and an ever-expanding dataset.
